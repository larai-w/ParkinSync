# ParkinSync Delivery Model

ParkinSync is managed as a solo, AI-assisted, evidence-led product and research engineering project. This repository does not claim a formal Scrum team or ceremonies that did not occur.

## Delivery flow

1. Frame a caregiver, operator, data-quality, or research outcome.
2. Write observable acceptance criteria and identify sensitive-data boundaries.
3. Record architecture and product tradeoffs before material cloud or schema changes.
4. Implement a small increment against anonymized fixtures where possible.
5. Retain test, CI, operator, or production-safe validation evidence.
6. Review privacy, recovery, and health-claims boundaries before release.

## Definition of ready

Work is ready when its outcome, acceptance criteria, validation plan, dependencies, and data risks are explicit. Research work must also state the hypothesis or question and avoid presenting exploratory correlation as clinical evidence.

## Definition of done

Work is done when the acceptance criteria are demonstrated, relevant tests pass, operational documentation is current, sensitive artifacts remain outside the public repository, and material production changes have a recovery path.

## Portfolio evidence

The public [ParkinSync Delivery project](https://github.com/users/larai-w/projects/4) connects work to priorities, phases, architecture areas, risks, and outcomes. Retrospective items are labelled `evidence:backfill` and linked to contemporaneous pull requests. New work is managed live through Issue Forms and pull requests.

## Product and research boundaries

ParkinSync supports caregiver record processing, data quality, and exploratory pattern analysis. It does not diagnose, recommend treatment, or claim that an observed association is causal. Human review remains part of OCR and publication workflows.
